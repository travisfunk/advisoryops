How scoring works

AdvisoryOps uses a deterministic, healthcare-aware scoring system. Each issue receives points based on observable evidence — source authority, exploit status, attack vector, healthcare context, and patch availability. The system does not rely on AI judgment for scoring; every point is traceable to a specific evidence factor.

Source authority tiers

Each source is assigned an authority tier from T1 (highest) to T5 (lowest) based on its institutional mandate, editorial rigor, and proximity to the vulnerability disclosure process. Higher-tier sources contribute more points to an issue's score.

T1 Government / Regulatory CISA KEV, CISA ICS-CERT, FDA
T2 Coordinated Disclosure CERT/CC, NVD, vendor SIRTs
T3 Security Research Claroty Team82, Nozomi, Dragos
T4 Curated News BleepingComputer, The Record, NCSC
T5 Community / Aggregator VulDB, social media, unverified feeds

How AI is used

EXTRACTION, NOT INVENTION
The AI extracts, normalizes, compares, and recommends from approved mitigation patterns. It does not generate novel security advice, diagnose vulnerabilities, or make clinical decisions. Every AI-generated field is labeled as such, and every recommendation is traceable to an approved playbook pattern with a cited basis.
EVIDENCE COMPLETENESS

Each issue is evaluated across 8 evidence fields: CVEs, summary, affected products, severity, vendor, patch status, mitigations, and source consensus. The evidence completeness score reflects how many of these fields contain meaningful data from authoritative sources.

What is AdvisoryOps?

AdvisoryOps is a deterministic, healthcare-focused vulnerability intelligence pipeline. It continuously ingests advisories from government, vendor, and research sources — scores them for healthcare relevance and urgency — and produces structured, actionable issue records for security teams at hospitals and health systems.

Why this exists

Small and rural hospitals face the same cyber threats as large health systems but with a fraction of the security staff. A 25-bed critical-access hospital cannot afford a dedicated vulnerability analyst, yet its patient monitors, infusion pumps, and imaging systems carry the same CVEs as those in a 900-bed academic medical center. AdvisoryOps exists to close that gap — providing structured, prioritized intelligence that would otherwise require a full-time analyst to produce.
