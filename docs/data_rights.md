# Data Rights and Source Attribution

AdvisoryOps normalizes and republishes factual information from public government sources (CISA, FDA, NVD, NCSC) and publicly available RSS/Atom feeds. No copyrighted text is reproduced verbatim. The pipeline extracts structured facts (CVE identifiers, severity levels, affected products, patch status) and generates AI-written summaries that are transformative restatements of the original advisory content. All source URLs are cited and linked in the output so users can verify facts against the original publication.

This approach constitutes factual reporting and transformative use of public safety data. Government advisories from CISA, FDA, and NVD are published as public domain works of the United States government and carry no copyright restrictions. RSS and Atom feeds from security vendors, research organizations, and news outlets are published specifically for automated consumption. AdvisoryOps respects robots.txt, rate limits, and feed-level access controls. Sources that block automated access (HTTP 403) are disabled rather than circumvented.

AI-generated content (summaries, recommendations, healthcare classifications) is clearly labeled with `generated_by: "ai"` attribution in every output record. The AI does not fabricate advisories or invent vulnerabilities — it summarizes, classifies, and recommends from the set of facts extracted from published sources. All remediation recommendations are constrained to an approved playbook of patterns reviewed by healthcare security practitioners. Source authority tiers ensure that authoritative sources (CISA, FDA) take precedence over news reporting when facts conflict.
